### 1. Executive Summary
This proposal outlines the strategic establishment of a dual-purpose commercial maize milling and animal feed processing plant in Mbarara, Uganda. Leveraging Mbarara’s position as a regional hub for livestock and agriculture, the project is highly viable. Under current East African Community (EAC) and Ugandan fiscal policies, the venture is eligible for significant tax exemptions on capital imports, enhancing the project's Internal Rate of Return (IRR).

### 2. Regulatory & Tax Framework
Uganda, as a member of the EAC, adheres to the **EAC Customs Management Act**, which provides specific incentives for the agricultural sector to promote value addition.

#### 2.1. Import Duty Exemptions
*   **Eligibility:** Under the EAC Customs Management Act, **agricultural processing machinery** is classified as exempt from import duty. This includes specialized equipment for maize milling and the production of animal feeds.
*   **Scope:** The exemption applies to the "primary" machinery required for processing. Note that spare parts or non-specialized vehicles may attract standard duties unless specifically cleared under an investment license.

#### 2.2. Domestic Tax Incentives (VAT)
*   **Animal Feeds:** The Uganda Value Added Tax (VAT) Act has been amended to **exempt animal feeds and premixes** from VAT. This reduces the cost of production and makes the final product more competitive for local farmers.
*   **Crop Extension Services:** Any extension services provided to maize out-growers are also VAT-exempt.
*   **Caveat:** While feeds are exempt, certain raw materials like wheat grain may attract VAT (18%), though maize remains a primary staple with favorable treatment.

### 3. Business Incorporation & Compliance
To qualify for these exemptions, the entity must be formally registered with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB).

*   **Registration Fees:** 50,000 UGX.
*   **Stamp Duty:** 10,000 UGX.
*   **Post-Registration:** The company must obtain a Tax Identification Number (TIN) from the Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) to apply for duty waivers during the clearing process at the border/port.

#### 4.2. Market Pricing Estimations
While exact "off-the-shelf" prices in Uganda fluctuate based on shipping and origin (primarily sourced from China or India), current market signals for a **20-ton per day (tpd) automatic plant** range between **USD 45,000 and USD 85,000** (CIF Kampala/Mbarara), excluding local installation costs. Mini-plants (300kg/hr) are available for smaller-scale entry-level operations at significantly lower price points.

### 5. Strategic Market Considerations for Mbarara
Mbarara serves as the "dairy heartland" of Uganda, creating a localized, high-demand market for animal feed (maize bran and specialized pellets).

*   **Soybean Deficit:** Regional market signals indicate a deficit in soybean meal—a critical protein component for feed. It is recommended that the facility includes a soybean crusher or establishes a supply chain from neighboring districts/Tanzania to maintain feed quality.
*   **SPS Measures:** To export flour or feed within the EAC (e.g., to Rwanda or South Sudan), the facility must comply with **Sanitary and Phyto-Sanitary (SPS)** measures. This requires certification from the Uganda National Bureau of Standards (UNBS).

### 6. Recommendations
1.  **Investment License:** Apply for a Uganda Investment Authority (UIA) Investment License (minimum $50k for domestic investors / $250k for foreign) to secure additional tax holidays and easier access to industrial land in Mbarara.
2.  **Solar Integration:** Given the high power requirements (72kW–135kW), consider a hybrid solar-grid solution to mitigate the impact of fluctuating industrial electricity tariffs.
3.  **Direct Procurement:** Engage directly with manufacturers for the milling machinery to take advantage of the duty-free status, rather than buying through local middlemen who may have already factored in duty costs.

**Conclusion:** 
Establishing a milling and feed plant in Mbarara is highly supported by the current legal and tax framework. The project qualifies for import duty exemptions on machinery and VAT exemptions on animal feeds, providing a robust foundation for commercial success.
